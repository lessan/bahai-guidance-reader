How does the Counsellors' arm differ from Spiritual Assemblies — and what is meant by "the learned and the rulers"?

Bahá'u'lláh names two categories in the Kitáb-i-'Ahd: "Blessed are the rulers and the learned among the people of Bahá." Shoghi Effendi interpreted this passage in 1931: the "learned" are the Hands of the Cause and the teachers of the Faith; the "rulers" are the members of the Local, National, and International Houses of Justice. Spiritual Assemblies are the rulers — elected bodies that adjudicate, administer, and decide within their jurisdiction. Only the Universal House of Justice legislates in the covenantal sense. Counsellors and their Auxiliary Board Members (ABMs) are the learned: appointed advisors whose authority is moral and pedagogical rather than administrative, focused on propagation and protection of the Faith.

Two Arms, One Body

The phrase "the learned and the rulers" captures a deliberate design: the Administrative Order requires both functions to remain healthy. Spiritual Assemblies provide the institutional scaffolding — they set policy, resolve disputes, manage resources, and give the community its legal and moral coherence. Without this ruling arm, the community would lack the order necessary for sustained collective action.

The Counsellors supply what administration alone cannot provide. Their task is to inspire, to warn when the spirit of the Cause is at risk, and to carry the vision of expansion and consolidation into every cluster and neighbourhood. Because their authority does not rest on elected office, they can speak with a freedom that an assembly member, mindful of administrative consensus, might not always exercise. The Universal House of Justice's 29 November 1969 elucidation names the structural distinction: whereas the "rulers" function as "corporate bodies," the "learned" operate "primarily as individuals." The two arms thus complement each other rather than compete: one holds the structure, the other animates it.

How the Relationship Works in Practice

Counsellors attend major assembly consultations as invited advisors. Their presence enriches deliberation, but the decision remains the assembly's alone. An LSA member who receives counsel from a Counsellor is morally expected to weigh it seriously — but is not administratively required to follow it. This distinction is not a loophole; it is structural. Binding authority flows through elected, accountable bodies; moral and visionary guidance flows through appointed, learned ones. The UHJ compilation The Institution of the Counsellors regulates the mode of interaction: National and Local Spiritual Assemblies "cannot give any directions to Auxiliary Board members," and if an Assembly wishes an ABM to undertake a specific service, it must convey its request to the Counsellors.

At the cluster level, ABMs work directly with study circles, animators, and core-activity facilitators — nurturing the human resources on which growth depends. The ABM for protection fulfils a distinct role: monitoring threats to community unity and alerting assemblies when individual behaviour or external pressure could undermine the Covenant. Here again the pattern holds — the ABM advises and alerts; it is the assembly that acts.

At the apex, the International Teaching Centre (ITC) in Haifa coordinates the Continental Boards of Counsellors worldwide and works in close consultation with the Universal House of Justice. This proximity to the supreme governing body ensures that the learned arm remains aligned with the overall direction set by the rulers — a relationship of ongoing, structured consultation rather than subordination or rivalry.

Historical Roots

The Counsellors' arm did not arise fully formed. It descended from the Hands of the Cause of God, whom Shoghi Effendi appointed to propagate and protect the Faith. After the passing of the Guardian, the Universal House of Justice created the Continental Boards of Counsellors in 1968 and the ITC in 1973 to perpetuate and extend these functions into the future. Understanding this lineage clarifies why the Counsellors carry such spiritual weight even without administrative authority: they are the institutional heirs of individuals Shoghi Effendi described as the "Chief Stewards" of the embryonic World Order.
