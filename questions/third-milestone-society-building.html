What does it mean for a cluster to enter the "third milestone" — and why is that the threshold at which the society-building power of the Faith begins to be released?

Direct answer

A cluster has entered the third milestone when its programme of growth has matured to the point that "a system for extending, in centre after centre, a dynamic pattern of community life that can engage a people — men and women, youth and adults — in the work of their own spiritual and social transformation" has appeared. The phrase is the Universal House of Justice's, from the 29 December 2015 message, which named the third milestone for the first time. It is at this threshold — and not earlier — that the society-building power of the Faith begins to be released, because at the third milestone the cluster's life has acquired enough density and capacity to act outwardly on the surrounding society rather than only inwardly on itself.

What the third milestone is

The first two milestones, named in the 28 December 2010 message to the Counsellors' Conference, track the interior maturation of a cluster's programme of growth. The first milestone is the moment a "nascent programme for the sustained expansion and consolidation of the Faith" becomes perceptible — small numbers of residents progressing through the institute courses and sustaining a handful of core activities. The second is the launching of an intensive programme of growth — the "maturation of an ever-expanding, self-sustaining system for the spiritual edification of a population."

The third milestone is qualitatively different. The 29 December 2015 message frames it not as further internal consolidation but as the appearance of a system for propagating the pattern of community life from one centre of intense activity to another. "For the movement of a population to have come this far," the House of Justice writes, "demonstrates that the process which brought it about is strong enough to achieve and sustain a high degree of participation in all aspects of the capacity-building endeavour and manage the complexity entailed." By Riḍván 2015, around two hundred clusters had reached this level. The 30 December 2021 message launching the Nine Year Plan set the prospective goal of reaching this level of intensity in roughly 1,300 clusters by the conclusion of the Plan in 2031.

What this looks like operationally: hundreds or thousands of participants; coordinators working in teams; Auxiliary Board assistants appointed to single villages or neighbourhoods; the Local Spiritual Assembly engaging with the spiritual and material life of the whole locality; stirrings of social action and contribution to prevalent discourses of society claiming an increasing share of the friends' attention.

Why this threshold releases the society-building power

The phrase "society-building power of the Faith" is not a coinage of the 2010s. It is Shoghi Effendi's — drawn from Citadel of Faith, where the Guardian writes of the "society-building power which their Faith possesses." The 28 December 2010 message quotes the passage in full at its close. What changes from 2015 onward is not the phrase but the centring: the Guardian's anticipatory description becomes the operative aim of a twenty-five-year arc of Plans. The 30 December 2021 message launching the Nine Year Plan makes this explicit, framing the 2022–2046 series of Plans as the "means of releasing what the Guardian described as 'the society-building power' of the Faith."

Why the third milestone, and not earlier? The 29 December 2015 message identifies the operative reason directly. Below the third milestone, the cluster's institute and its programme of growth are still primarily addressing the Bahá’í community and immediate seekers; the work is consolidating itself. At the third milestone, a different kind of activity becomes possible: the cluster has so much capacity that it begins to act outwardly. The 29 December 2015 message names the decisive shift as the systematic concentration of activity in neighbourhoods and villages where "receptivity wells up" — small centres of population in which "the society-building power inherent in the Faith becomes most visible."

The 28 December 2010 message had already anticipated this outward turn — observing that as a cluster's diverse activities multiply, they begin to generate settings in which the friends and those around them engage together in the work of community life, and that the maturing Administrative Order itself begins to manifest the society-building power Shoghi Effendi described. The Riḍván 2012 message then drew the operational consequence: in five clusters whose growth had reached sufficient maturity — Matunda Soy, Battambang, Bihar Sharif, Norte del Cauca, and Tanna — "the emergence of a local Mashriqu'l-Adhkár can be contemplated." The third-milestone cluster is, by this reasoning, the cluster ready to host a House of Worship.

What it looks like on the ground: Norte del Cauca

A concrete case clarifies what "release of society-building power" means in practice. The Norte del Cauca cluster in Colombia — home of the Ruhi Institute and one of the five sites designated by the Riḍván 2012 message for a local Mashriqu'l-Adhkár — is documented across multiple issues of Reflections on Growth. By the time of R.O.G. 20 it had become a learning site whose resource persons travelled to clusters across Colombia, Costa Rica, Mexico, Bolivia, Peru and Ecuador; in one such case, R.O.G. 20 records "over 1,000 new believers" enrolled in a wave catalysed by Norte del Cauca-trained workers in the Cochabamba region of Bolivia. Its junior-youth programme, its devotional gatherings, and its educational work had reached a density at which the cluster's life was visibly distinct in the surrounding population.

Three things are simultaneously happening in such a cluster: a local House of Worship (built or in formation) becoming the spiritual centre of community life; social-action initiatives — Bahá’í-inspired schools, agricultural programmes, conflict-reconciliation work — operating at scale; and engagement with local civic institutions on questions of education, health and well-being. None of these is bolted onto a programme of growth as an extra; each arises from the density of community life that the third milestone marks.

A caution: M3 is a way of life, not a number

The 29 December 2015 message is insistent that the third milestone is read, not awarded — and that its quantitative correlates (cluster size, declarations per cycle) are "more visible signs of a much finer development: the gradual spread, within a population, of a pattern of community life based on Bahá’u’lláh’s teachings." The third milestone is not equivalent to mass conversion; it is the appearance of a way of life that is recognisable, attractive, and capable of being extended. The 30 December 2021 message warns against turning the framework into a checklist by reminding the friends that "it is not the provision of training by itself that brings about progress."

The Nine Year Plan's specific sub-goal — "that during the Nine Year Plan the process of growth reach this level of intensity in at least one cluster in every country and every region" (30 December 2021) — treats the third-milestone cluster as a "reservoir" of knowledge and pioneers for the surrounding region. That logic, too, presupposes that what is being propagated is a pattern of life, not a numerical achievement.
