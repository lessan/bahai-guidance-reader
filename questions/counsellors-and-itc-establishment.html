When were the Continental Boards of Counsellors established, and when was the International Teaching Centre established?

The Universal House of Justice established the eleven Continental Boards of Counsellors on 21 June 1968 by cable to all National Spiritual Assemblies. Five years later, on 5 June 1973, the House announced by cable the establishment of the International Teaching Centre in the Holy Land, with a detailed letter following on 8 June; the inaugural meeting was held in Haifa on 14 June 1973. Both institutions emerged from a single problem: the functions of the Hands of the Cause of God — protection and propagation — were too vital to allow them to lapse, yet no further Hands could be appointed once the line of Guardians had ended.

Antecedents: the Hands of the Cause of God

The Hands of the Cause were instituted by Bahá’u’lláh and charged, in the sacred Texts, with "the specific duties of protecting and propagating the Faith." ’Abdu’l-Bahá gave the institution its mandate in the Will and Testament, directing that the Hands operate under the Guardian's guidance and "diffuse the Divine Fragrances, edify the souls of men, promote learning, improve the character of all men." Shoghi Effendi named fifty Hands in all — the last contingents appointed between 1951 and 1957 — and styled them "Chief Stewards of Bahá’u’lláh’s embryonic World Commonwealth."

When Shoghi Effendi passed in November 1957, the twenty-seven surviving Hands convened at Bahjí, designated nine of their number as Custodians of the Holy Land, and administered the Bahá’í world — completing the Ten Year Crusade, overseeing the formation of new National Assemblies, repelling a Covenant-breaking attempt — until 7 June 1963, when they relinquished all authority to the newly elected Universal House of Justice. That six-year custodianship demonstrated the institution's capacity and its indispensability; it also made the succession question urgent. In 1963 and again in 1964 the House of Justice found, after careful study, that it possessed no authority to appoint further Hands. The functions, however, could not be left to extinguish with the surviving Hands themselves.

The Continental Boards of Counsellors (1968)

The Nine Year Plan (1964–1973) included as one of its foundational goals the "development of the Institution of the Hands with a view to extension into the future of its appointed functions of protection and propagation." For four years the House of Justice consulted with the surviving Hands on how this could be done. The 24 June 1968 letter To the Bahá’ís of the World named the structural constraint plainly:

The Universal House of Justice sees no way in which additional Hands of the Cause of God can be appointed. The absence of the Guardian of the Faith brought about an entirely new relationship between the Universal House of Justice and the Hands of the Cause and called for the progressive unfoldment by the Universal House of Justice of the manner in which the Hands of the Cause would carry out their divinely conferred functions of protection and propagation.

— Universal House of Justice, 24 June 1968

The cable of 21 June 1968 announced the solution:

Rejoice announce momentous decision establish eleven Continental Boards Counselors protection propagation Faith three each for Africa Americas Asia one each for Australasia Europe. Adoption this significant step following consultation with Hands Cause God ensures extension future appointed functions their institution.

— Universal House of Justice, cable, 21 June 1968

Eleven Boards, reflecting the Hands' own zonal assignments, came into being that day. The Hands themselves were not retired; they were released from the administration of the Auxiliary Boards so they could devote their remaining energies to the "preservation of the spiritual health of the Bahá’í communities" and the "vitality of the faith" of believers worldwide — the intercontinental and spiritual dimension that their unique station had always made their primary contribution. What the Counsellors inherited was the institutional framework: they directed the Auxiliary Boards (previously the Hands' deputies), carried the functions of protection and propagation forward, and were appointed by the Universal House of Justice for renewable five-year terms.

The character of the role as it passed from Hands to Counsellors was explicitly non-authoritative. The 2001 document The Institution of the Counsellors records the principle that defined both institutions: the Counsellors and those they direct possess "no legislative, executive or judicial authority, and are entirely devoid of priestly functions or the right to make authoritative interpretations." Counsel — its weight conferred by the appointing body, not by coercive power — is the method.

The International Teaching Centre (1973)

The Counsellors quickly proved that a further development was needed: the eleven continental Boards lacked a coordinating centre at the World Centre, and the Hands resident in Haifa held long experience in the global direction of teaching work that was at risk of being lost. The House of Justice's cable of 5 June 1973 proclaimed:

Announce establishment Holy Land long anticipated International Teaching Center destined evolve into one those world-shaking world-embracing world-directing administrative institutions ordained by Bahá’u’lláh anticipated by ’Abdu’l-Bahá elucidated by Shoghi Effendi. Membership this nascent institution comprises all Hands Cause God and initially three Counselors who with Hands present Holy Land will constitute nucleus its vital operations.

— Universal House of Justice, cable, 5 June 1973

The 8 June letter followed, explaining that the establishment of the International Teaching Centre simultaneously brought "to fruition the work of the Hands of the Cause residing in the Holy Land" and provided "for its extension into the future" — it linked the Counsellors more intimately with the Hands and "powerfully reinforced the discharge of the rapidly growing responsibilities of the Universal House of Justice." The inaugural meeting of the Centre was held at Haifa on 14 June 1973, with the Hands of the Cause resident in the Holy Land — Rúḥíyyih Khánum, Abu'l-Qásim Faizí, 'Alí-Akbar Furútan, and Paul Haney — joined by the three inaugural Counsellors: Hooper Dunbar, Florence Mayberry, and 'Azíz Yazdí.

All Hands of the Cause living elsewhere in the world held permanent membership in the Centre for life; over the following decades, as the Hands passed away and Counsellor membership expanded — reaching nine members in 1988, the number settled since — the Teaching Centre became fully Counsellor-composed. From 1984 until early 2001 it operated from 10 Haparsim Street, Haifa; on 14 January 2001 it occupied its permanent seat on the Arc on Mount Carmel.

The Teaching Centre's function is coordinative and analytical. It coordinates, stimulates, and directs the Continental Boards of Counsellors; gathers and synthesises what is being learned in the field — today, chiefly in clusters worldwide; channels that learning upward to the House of Justice and outward through the Counsellors to the believers. It does not legislate, does not interpret the Texts, and cannot override UHJ guidance; its authority is conferred and executive.

The Auxiliary Board structure

The Auxiliary Boards preceded both the Counsellors and the Teaching Centre. Shoghi Effendi asked each Hand of the Cause to appoint deputies in 1954; in October 1957 he established a second Auxiliary Board on each continent, so that one would serve Protection and another Propagation. This dual structure has continued under the Counsellors since 1968. The Constitution of the Universal House of Justice (1972) gives it statutory form: in each zone "there shall be two Auxiliary Boards, one for the protection and one for the propagation of the Faith," whose members "shall serve under the direction of the Continental Boards of Counsellors and shall act as their deputies, assistants and advisers."

A further extension came on 7 October 1973: the Universal House of Justice authorised Auxiliary Board members to appoint assistants to serve at the locality level — activating Local Spiritual Assemblies, encouraging regular Feast and Holy Day observance, and deepening believers' understanding of the Teachings. The "cluster" as a planning unit did not exist until 2001; the 1973 assistants were deployed in service of local communities and Assemblies, not cluster agencies. As the cluster vocabulary matured from the 9 January 2001 message onward, assistants increasingly served within the emerging cluster framework — a development The Institution of the Counsellors (2001) systematises, noting that "intimate involvement in institute operations is a part of the evolving functions of Auxiliary Board members." The document is precise on designation: "The correct designation is 'assistants to the Auxiliary Board members' and not 'assistants to the Auxiliary Board'." The result is a four-tier cascade — House of Justice → Counsellors → Auxiliary Board members → assistants — through which the guidance of the centre reaches the grassroots.

The 2001 elaboration

On 1 January 2001, as the International Teaching Centre moved into its permanent seat on the Arc, the Universal House of Justice issued The Institution of the Counsellors — the consolidated and definitive account of the institution's history, structure, and mode of operation. The document's opening notes that "more than three decades have passed since the establishment, in 1968, of the Continental Boards of Counsellors" and proceeds to systematise what three decades of experience had taught.

Several relationships that had developed in practice were now given authoritative form. The relationship of the Teaching Centre to the Continental Boards: the Centre "coordinates, stimulates and directs the activities of the Continental Boards of Counsellors" and acts as liaison between them and the House. The relationship of Counsellors to National Spiritual Assemblies: "one of loving cooperation between two institutions of the Faith that are serving the same ends" — the Counsellors occupy a rank higher than the Assemblies, ensuring their advice is attended to, but cannot direct or override elected bodies. The role of Auxiliary Board members with the emerging cluster agencies: "intimate involvement in institute operations is a part of the evolving functions of Auxiliary Board members." And the mode that governs all of it: Counsellors "stimulate and advise; they do not direct or command."

The document also addressed the question of what happens when the institution of the Hands has fully passed from the scene — a reality that arrived with the passing of the last Hand, 'Alí-Muhammad Varqá, in 2007. The answer had been structurally prepared over four decades: the functions of protection and propagation, the coordination of those functions at the world centre, the cascade of Boards and assistants into every locality — all of it now flows entirely through the institution of the Counsellors, the Teaching Centre, and the Auxiliary Boards.
