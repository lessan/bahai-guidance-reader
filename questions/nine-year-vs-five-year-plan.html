How does the Nine Year Plan (2022–2031) differ from the Five Year Plan (2016–2021)?

The Five Year Plan (2016–2021) called for “a clear arc of progress over the course of twenty cycles” and aimed to “raise the number of clusters where a programme of growth has become intensive to 5,000 by Riḍván 2021.” The Nine Year Plan (2022–2031) opens the first major undertaking in a twenty-five-year arc to Riḍván 2046, with the overarching aim of “the release of the society-building power of the Faith in ever-greater measures.” Both Plans share the cluster-and-institute architecture; the shift is a re-centring on society-building as the quarter-century telos, not an abandonment of community-building.

From Community-Building to Society-Building

The FYP already extended learning “beyond the realm of expansion and consolidation to encompass other areas of Bahá’í endeavour, notably social action and participation in the prevalent discourses of society.” Riḍván 2021 reported that “the society-building power of the Faith of Bahá’u’lláh is being manifested with ever more clarity” — a foundation for what followed.

The 30 December 2021 message charters the shift: a twenty-five-year series to Riḍván 2046 focused on releasing society-building power through three familiar areas of endeavour — vibrant outward-looking communities, spiritual and material progress, and contribution to prevalent discourses. The number of places where a Bahá’í pattern of community life has attained wide prevalence remains “modest, yet it is growing.”

Riḍván 2022 extends this: the friends are “outward looking in the process of community building” and, in rising numbers, carry “a sense of real responsibility for the spiritual and material progress of larger and larger groups within society.” Community-building remains the grassroots method; society-building is the horizon.

Scale and Phase Structure

The FYP‘s single numerical goal — 5,000 intensive programmes — is not directly comparable to the NYP’s three milestone projections. At Riḍván 2021 the community achieved approximately 4,000 intensive programmes; roughly 1,000 clusters had passed the third milestone — an outcome the message had hoped for, not a Plan target.

The NYP’s headline numbers were fixed at Riḍván 2022, not in the 30 December 2021 charter. After national re-clustering — a definitional exercise, not organic growth — the total “has risen by a quarter and now stands at over 22,000.” Against that denominator, Riḍván 2022 projected programmes of growth in around 14,000 clusters, intensive activity in 11,000, and more than 5,000 past the third milestone by 2031 — “worthy aspirations towards which to strive.” The 30 December 2021 forecasts were “only intended to be well-informed estimates.” See nine-year-plan-goals-tracking.

Riḍván 2022 also named a two-phase structure — “two phases of four and five years’ duration” — with advances expected by Riḍván 2026 and then Riḍván 2031. The 30 December 2021 letter did not use this phasing.

New Emphases: Mashriqu’l-Adhkár, Conversation, and Noble Characteristics

Three elements receive sharper NYP attention.

Local Mashriqu’l-Adhkárs. Riḍván 2022 situates the sixth epoch of the Formative Age around learning “the path that leads from the development” of embryonic local Houses of Worship — building on continental and national projects already under way.

Meaningful conversation. The 29 December 2015 message already described “the rising capacity for meaningful conversation” in second-milestone clusters. The 30 December 2021 message develops teaching through sustained dialogue; the NYP intensifies rather than invents this mode.

Noble characteristics and three dimensions. Riḍván 2022 points to “communities with the noble characteristics we described in the 30 December 2021 message” — resilience, rationality, conduct, compassion, detachment, forbearance. The same Riḍván message describes community-building, social action, and discourses as “one global enterprise, bound together by a common framework for action.”
