Meeting Preparation Guide

A practical framework for using this knowledge base to prepare for Bahá’í community meetings, whether at the cluster, regional, or national level. Covers reflection meetings, ATC meetings, institutional gatherings, and conferences. The live Plan is the Nine Year Plan (2022–2031); serious questions on consultation and reflection-meeting practice are routed back to the saturated Consultation and ReflectionMeeting pages.

Types of meetings and what to prepare

1. Cluster Reflection Meetings

Purpose: Review the cycle of growth just completed; plan the next cycle; build shared understanding of the community's direction.

Preparation questions:

  • What happened in the last cycle? (Numbers: study circles started/completed, JY groups, children's classes, devotionals, teaching activities)
  • What did we learn? (Not just what happened, but what does it tell us about our capacities, the receptivity of our community, what we should adjust)
  • What does the guidance from the Plan say about our stage of development? → See cluster-development-framework
  • What are the goals for the next cycle? (Targets for each type of activity; specific neighbourhoods)
  • Who are the key human resources? (Tutors, animators, teachers, facilitators)

Key wiki pages: ReflectionMeeting, cluster-development-framework, three-milestones, NineYearPlan, ProgramOfGrowth

2. Area Teaching Committee Meetings

Purpose: Coordinate activity across the cluster; ensure alignment with the Plan's goals; support individual communities.

Preparation:

  • State of each community in the cluster (milestone progress)
  • Human resources map: who is trained in what, where are the gaps
  • Priority neighbourhoods for the current cycle
  • Teaching dynamics: who is visiting homes, what conversations are happening
  • Any social action initiatives emerging

Key wiki pages: cluster-development-framework, three-milestones, social-action-guidance, InstituteProcess

3. Training Institute Events

Purpose: Run courses; develop tutors, animators, teachers; build the culture of learning.

Key questions:

  • Which books are most needed? (Often Book 7 — the tutor gateway — is the bottleneck)
  • Who should be prioritised for the next sequence? (Young people; people in high-potential neighbourhoods)
  • Are the "requisite attitudes and skills" being developed, not just information transferred?

Key wiki pages: InstituteProcess

4. Regional/National Conferences

Purpose: Large-scale consultation on the direction of the Plan; learning across multiple clusters; inspiration and capacity building.

Preparation:

  • Overall trajectory of the region/nation: cluster growth statistics
  • Best practices emerging from high-growth clusters
  • Specific challenges being faced
  • Questions from the UHJ or Counsellors that need exploration

Key wiki pages: NineYearPlan, nine-year-plan-2022, cluster-development-framework

5. Social/Civic Gatherings (not Bahá’í-specific)

Purpose: Engagement with society; sharing Bahá’í insights in professional or civic contexts.

Preparation:

  • What do the Bahá’í teachings say about this topic? → Use `/wiki-query`
  • Key quotes from authoritative sources that speak to the topic
  • Avoid positioning as "the Bahá’í solution" — instead, contribute insights to a shared inquiry

Key wiki pages: social-action-guidance, BahaiInternationalCommunity

The consultation framework

All Bahá’í meetings use consultation as their method of collective decision-making. The saturated treatment is at Consultation; the principal teachings are gathered in the Research Department's compilation Consultation (1978, rev. 1990). The operative principles for meeting practice:

  1. Detachment from positions: Once an idea is offered, it ceases to be the speaker's property; the "shining spark of truth cometh forth only after the clash of differing opinions" (’Abdu’l-Bahá, Compilation on Consultation, item 9).
  2. Unity as the medium of truth: ’Abdu’l-Bahá teaches that united agreement on a wrong decision can be revisited and corrected, while a fractured insistence on a right one cannot — "if they agree and both parties are in the wrong, as it is in unity the truth will be revealed and the wrong made right" (Compilation on Consultation, item 12). Unity is the precondition for self-correction, not its enemy.
  3. Frank but courteous: Members must have "the courage of their convictions" (Shoghi Effendi, 28 October 1935), expressing their views openly while never belittling another's.
  4. No canvassing: Influencing others outside the meeting room is contrary to the spirit of consultation; secrecy "is most alien to the spirit of the Cause" (Shoghi Effendi, 28 October 1935).
  5. Learning orientation: The purpose is to find truth, not to win arguments — "the ideal of Bahá’í consultation is to arrive at a unanimous decision" (UHJ, 6 March 1970, The Spirit of Bahá’í Consultation); when unanimity cannot be reached, the majority decision becomes the decision of the whole.

Relevant quotations for meeting prep

On reflection and learning

"The coherence of the enterprise depends on the ability of its participants to learn — to act, to reflect, to capture insights, and to absorb the insights emerging elsewhere." — UHJ, Riḍván 2022

On community building in the neighbourhood

"A determination to be outward looking in the process of community building had already become an established aspect of culture in many, many places; it has now blossomed, in a rising number of communities, into a sense of real responsibility for the spiritual and material progress of larger and larger groups within society." — UHJ, Riḍván 2022

On the purpose of the programme of growth

"Every cluster where the activities of the Plan are gaining momentum, we see the development of communities with noble characteristics... followers of the Abhá Beauty must stand out more and more for their qualities of resilience and rationality, for their standard of conduct and their adherence to principle, and for the compassion, detachment, and forbearance they demonstrate in their pursuit of unity." — UHJ, Riḍván 2022

On the role of training institutes

"A sequence of courses that seeks to build capacity for service by concentrating on the application of the spiritual insights gained through profound study of the Writings." — UHJ, 27 December 2005

On junior youth

"The Bahá’í community — in the language it employs and the approaches it adopts — is moving decidedly in the opposite direction, seeing in junior youth instead altruism, an acute sense of justice, eagerness to learn about the universe and a desire to contribute to the construction of a better world." — UHJ, Riḍván 2010
