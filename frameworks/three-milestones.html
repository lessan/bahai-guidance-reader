Three Milestones of Cluster Development

Orientation

The “three milestones” framework is the Universal House of Justice's current way of describing the development of a Cluster. Each milestone names a qualitative threshold that community-building work in a geographic area has crossed — not a status conferred by an institution, but a change in the character of activity readable from the ground. The framework replaced an earlier scheme of four categories (informally A/B/C/D) in use from roughly 2001 to 2010, which had ranked clusters by the prior strength of the Bahá’í community already present. The milestone framing turns the question around: what matters is not how many Bahá’ís live in a cluster but whether a self-sustaining, population-wide process of capacity building has taken root, and how far it has advanced.

Each milestone poses a distinct operative question. First milestone: has a nascent programme for the sustained expansion and consolidation of the Faith become discernible — in however small a form? Second milestone: has that programme matured into an intensive, self-sustaining system for the spiritual edification of a population? Third milestone: has the system learned to embrace large numbers, sustain complexity, and begin to release the Faith's society-building power — such that a pattern of community life is spreading through the population itself? These questions tell cluster agencies what the work of the moment actually is, and they structure the goal-setting of each successive Plan.

The framework is dynamic rather than diagnostic. A milestone is a passage, not a badge; clusters can regress; progress is organic and uneven. The House of Justice has been insistent that the milestones name a continuum of development, and that much of what matters happens between them.

Narrative spine

The anchor text is the 28 December 2010 message from the Universal House of Justice to the Conference of the Continental Boards of Counsellors — the letter that launched what would become the 2011–2016 Five Year Plan. It was in that message that the four-stage classification carried over from the earlier Plans was explicitly set aside in favour of a “rich and dynamic continuum,” and that the first two milestones were named. The House of Justice observed that after a decade of experience, “the abundant experience which has since accrued enables the believers now to conceive of the movement of a population, propelled by mounting spiritual forces, in terms of a rich and dynamic continuum.” The first milestone was identified as the moment when an “initial flow of human resources into the field of systematic action” becomes perceptible — when, in whatever combination and however small in number, devotional gatherings, children's classes and junior youth groups are being sustained by those progressing through institute courses, such that a “nascent programme for the sustained expansion and consolidation of the Faith can be perceived.” The second milestone was the “launching” of an IntensiveProgramOfGrowth — “conscious recognition that all the elements necessary to accelerate the expansion and consolidation of the Faith are not only in place but also functioning with an adequate degree of effectiveness.” The passage named this a “self-sustaining system for the spiritual edification of a population.” (A small textual note: 28 December 2010 introduces M1 as “the first of several milestones” but names M2 only as “another milestone that the friends labouring in every cluster must, in time, reach”; the explicit second-milestone numbering is applied retroactively by the 29 December 2015 message, which refers back to “the second milestone along the continuum of development, which we described to you five years ago.”)

The 29 December 2015 message — introducing what would become the final Five Year Plan of the first century of the Formative Age — added the third milestone. Reflecting back on 2010, the House of Justice wrote that “a collective understanding of what is required for the friends in a cluster to pass the first of the milestones we described, and then the second, has grown over this period.” It then described a further threshold: the appearance of “a system for extending, in centre after centre, a dynamic pattern of community life that can engage a people — men and women, youth and adults — in the work of their own spiritual and social transformation.” By 2015, around two hundred clusters had reached this level.

The 30 December 2021 message (launching the Nine Year Plan) is the first to treat all three milestones as established vocabulary needing no re-explanation: “The features of the developmental path that should be followed, and in particular the first, second, and third milestones that mark progress along the way, are already well known to the friends from our previous messages and from their own experience.” That message set numerical goals keyed directly to the milestones and introduced the headline objective of establishing a programme of growth in every cluster in the world. It also makes the framework's underlying claim explicit by drawing the chain back to the Guardian: the 2022–2046 series of Plans, the message states, is focused on “the release of what the Guardian described as 'the society-building power' of the Faith.” That phrase, originating in Shoghi Effendi's writings on the unfolding of the Administrative Order, is the Tier-B anchor that the milestone framework's third-stage language finally surfaces by name.

Between these anchor letters sit the Riḍván messages reporting progress, the 27 December 2005 message that coined the “two movements” image the milestones refine, and the Reflections on Growth issues — especially 28, 32, 33, and 35 — which document how cluster agencies translate the milestone vocabulary into the practical question of what to work on next. The International Teaching Centre's Insights from the Frontiers of Learning (2013) draws out the operational implications of the milestone framework as an empirical synthesis of cluster experience — what nascent capacities a programme of growth requires, how the institute process roots itself, what the second-to-third passage looks like on the ground; the UHJ messages remain the doctrinal source.

The milestone thresholds are qualitative but they correspond to visible quantitative changes: a rise in the number of those in meaningful conversations, homes being visited, core-activity participants, and individuals beginning the sequence. The House of Justice has stressed repeatedly that “such advances are the more visible signs of a much finer development: the gradual spread, within a population, of a pattern of community life based on Bahá’u’lláh’s teachings” (29 Dec 2015).

Thresholds and movement between milestones

Movement from one milestone to the next is organic and has no fixed timetable. Some clusters traverse the whole continuum in a few years; others remain at the first for a Plan or more. What the House of Justice's messages have progressively clarified is what conditions each transition requires.

The passage from first to second is largely a question of the institute process becoming rooted locally. In the earliest phase most tutors might come from neighbouring clusters; the transition cannot be sustained without “the first cadre of tutors from the cluster” being raised up (29 Dec 2015). Capacity must appear from within the population. Administrative structures — an Area Teaching Committee, an institute coordinator team, quarterly reflection meetings — emerge in response to real need rather than being imposed in advance; the House of Justice warns against both “acting prematurely” and “unduly delaying the appearance of formal structures.” The movement requires help from outside — the “second movement” of human resources into the cluster, described in rog-25-accelerating-the-second-movement — without that help displacing the initiative of local residents.

The passage from second to third is more demanding and more distinctive. A cluster can run an intensive programme of growth for years without crossing it. What the 29 December 2015 message identified as the decisive shift is the systematic concentration of activity in neighbourhoods and villages where “receptivity wells up” — small centres of population where the interlocking dimensions of community life are “most coherently expressed” and where “the society-building power inherent in the Faith becomes most visible.” The third milestone is reached when this pattern has established itself broadly enough that the cluster as a whole has developed a system for extending a dynamic pattern of community life from one centre of intense activity to another. Complexity becomes the main challenge: hundreds or thousands of participants, coordinators in teams, assistants appointed to single villages, a Local Spiritual Assembly engaging with authorities and with the spiritual education of an entire generation.

Beyond the third milestone lies a further horizon that the 30 December 2021 message describes without naming as a fourth milestone: the release of society-building power in places where a “significant proportion of the population” is engaged in community-building activities and the Bahá’í community “has become a highly visible protagonist in society in its own right.” This is framed as a new frontier of learning rather than a new threshold; the messages are careful that such prevalence “is not a prospect everywhere.”

Throughout, the House of Justice has insisted on the danger of turning the framework into a checklist. “It is not the provision of training by itself that brings about progress” (29 Dec 2015). Milestones are read, not awarded. The cluster agencies' task is to help the friends “read their own reality” and identify the immediate next steps.

Relationship to other frameworks

To the earlier A/B/C/D classification. The milestone framework supersedes the four strength-based categories of 2001–2010 (see Cluster for the full classification-evolution table). The older scheme measured the strength of the Bahá’í community already present and ranked clusters against readiness to launch an intensive programme of growth; the milestone scheme measures the qualitative movement of a population, for which the size of the prior Bahá’í community is secondary. The 28 December 2010 message explicitly framed the shift as a move from stages to a continuum.

To the three protagonists. Each milestone corresponds to a deepening of the protagonists' mutual relationship: at the first the individual predominates; at the second, institutional structures (Area Teaching Committee, institute coordinators, Local Assembly) come into their own; at the third, the community itself emerges as a visible protagonist capable of sustaining a pattern of life.

To the three dimensions of Bahá’í endeavour. The three dimensions — expansion, involvement in the life of society, and contribution to prevalent discourses — become progressively more balanced as clusters advance. At the first and second milestones, expansion and consolidation predominates. At the third, social action and discourse claim an increasing share of the friends' attention.

To the phases of the NineYearPlan. The Plan (2022–2031) is structured around milestone thresholds. Its objective is to establish an intensive programme of growth in every cluster in the world — pushing every cluster past the first milestone and, increasingly, past the second. A specific sub-goal is that at least one third-milestone cluster exist in every country and region, so its experience can radiate outward — a strategic use of the “second movement” of human resources.

Global progression of clusters by milestone

The House of Justice periodically reports milestone counts, typically in messages introducing a new Plan. Because the vocabulary itself was settled only in 2010 and elaborated in 2015, consistent counts exist from roughly 2010 onward; earlier figures used the language of “intensive programmes of growth” rather than milestones, but map onto the second milestone.

The numbers are less important than what they indicate about the trajectory the House of Justice is projecting: the second milestone is expected to become the baseline condition of every cluster on earth within the current series of Plans, and the third milestone to become ordinary rather than exceptional.
