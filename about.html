About this site

Bahá'í Guidance Reader is a collection of research summaries — questions, frameworks, and syntheses — grounded in the authoritative Bahá'í corpus. Each article opens with a direct answer, then weaves quotations from the Writings, guidance from the Universal House of Justice, and (where appropriate) field examples from Reflections on Growth.

These pages are for study and meeting preparation. They are not official Bahá'í guidance. Verify any quotation at bahai.org. When in doubt, consult your Local Spiritual Assembly.

For family and friends

Browse the Questions if you are preparing for a feast, children's class, study circle, or a conversation about the Faith. If you cannot find what you need, use Ask a question — submissions are reviewed, and new articles are written and published over time.

For Bahá'ís building with AI

If you are using AI tools to support community work — tutoring, animating, serving on institutions, preparing deepenings — this section describes what was built and how, in case the architecture is useful for your own projects.

What this is

A query-driven intelligence system over a curated Bahá'í corpus:

  1. Acquisition — monitors and downloaders collect authoritative texts, institutional messages, news, Ruhi materials, and scholarly sources into `raw/`.
  2. Conversion — everything becomes searchable markdown in `sources/` (the citation surface; not redistributed on this site).
  3. Intelligence layer — concepts, frameworks, a 100+ question bank, syntheses, and time-series data in structured wiki pages.
  4. Publish layer — a subset of those pages, polished and link-checked, becomes this static site.

There is no database and no public chatbot. The value is pre-computed, tier-disciplined synthesis that makes grounded answers cheap to produce in local Claude sessions.

Quality model

  • Authority tiers (A–F): Writings and the Báb at the top; UHJ and Guardian next; ITC, R.O.G., and scholarly sources explicitly labelled — never cited above their tier.
  • Authenticity pass: every publishable page checked for verbatim quotations, composite-quote risks, and misattributed authority.
  • Style: Ruhi-methodology interleaving — short attributed quotations woven with narrative, not walls of excerpt.
  • External links: readers are pointed to bahai.org for primary texts, not secondary mirrors.

What is not shared

  • The full `sources/` corpus (~23k files) — copyright and size make public redistribution inappropriate.
  • Raw acquisition tooling as a hosted service — the pipeline runs locally.
  • Instant public Q&A — answers are editorially produced, not streamed from a model.

Replication

High bar: building your own corpus, intelligence layer, and authenticity discipline.

Lower bar: read and share these articles; suggest questions via the form; adapt the publish scripts (`tools/publish/` in the private knowledge-base repo) for your own markdown corpus.

If you are experimenting with AI for Bahá'í community work, the durable lesson is: the moat is tier discipline and citation verification, not the model. A smaller, verified synthesis layer outperforms a raw RAG dump every time.
